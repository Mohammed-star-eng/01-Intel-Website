Eco-Engineered Futures: Intel's Journey to Net Zero

Intel’s journey started with a spark of innovation — and it hasn’t stopped since. From early breakthroughs to bold green goals, we’ve been building a future that’s smarter, cleaner, and more connected. The road so far? Just the beginning.

1968 – Foundation

In the early days of chip manufacturing, Intel emphasized reducing materials waste and efficient energy use.

1983 – Water Recycling Starts

One of the first major tech companies to invest in water recycling systems, cutting usage by millions of gallons annually.

1995 – Green Building Design

Fabs were redesigned to use natural light, passive cooling, and eco-materials long before LEED standards were popularized.

2003 – First Renewable Energy Goals

Started with 10% clean energy goal, later increased to over 70% across its campuses globally by 2020.

2008 – Carbon Transparency

Joined Carbon Disclosure Project, pushing supply chain partners to follow transparency standards too.

2015 – Zero Waste to Landfill

Intel facilities begin diverting more than 90% of solid waste from landfills through reuse, recycling, and composting.

2020 – Net Zero Labs Launched

Intel’s R&D campuses begin operating with net-zero carbon emissions through solar, energy recapture, and battery tech.

2022 – AI for Energy Efficiency

Machine learning models cut electricity use in chipmaking tools by over 15% annually.

2025 – Future Vision

Committing to circular economy principles and further innovation in sustainable materials and processes.
